Planetary

Embark on a journey to explore the unique wonders, mysteries, and science of the planets in our solar system and beyond.

The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system, providing the energy that sustains life on Earth.

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun. It is known for its extreme temperatures and cratered surface.

Venus

Venus, often called Earth's twin, is shrouded in thick clouds of sulfuric acid, with a surface temperature hot enough to melt lead.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life, with diverse ecosystems and a perfect balance of atmosphere, water, and temperature.

Mars

Mars, the Red Planet, is home to the tallest volcano and the largest canyon in the solar system. It has been a focal point for exploration.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, known for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm larger than Earth.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system, made up of ice and rock particles, and is the second-largest planet in our solar system.

Uranus

Uranus, an ice giant, rotates on its side and has a pale blue color due to methane in its atmosphere.

Neptune

Neptune, the farthest planet from the Sun, is known for its deep blue color and supersonic winds.
